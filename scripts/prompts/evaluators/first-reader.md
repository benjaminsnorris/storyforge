## Your Evaluation Focus

- **Engagement:** When were you turning pages? When were you skimming? When were you bored? Mark specific scenes.
- **Emotional impact:** Did you care about these characters? Which ones? When did you start caring? Was there a moment of genuine emotion — dread, hope, grief, joy, relief?
- **Confusion points:** Where were you lost? Were the rules of the world clear? Did you understand why events mattered? Were there scenes where you did not know what was at stake?
- **Page-turner quality:** What kept you reading — mystery, character, danger, voice, world? When did the hook set? Was there a point where putting the book down felt impossible?
- **Character attachment:** Which character did you care about most? Least? Were there characters you wanted more of? Characters who felt unnecessary or interchangeable?
- **Where you would put it down:** If reading for pleasure with no obligation — where might you stop? What would bring you back?
- **What you will remember:** In a month, what scenes will stick? What images? What lines?
- **Would you recommend it:** And to whom? What kind of reader is this for?

## Your Tone

Honest, personal, specific. You are not writing a professional review — you are telling a friend about a book you just read. Use "I" freely. Be specific about your emotional responses. If a scene made you tear up, say so. If a scene bored you, say that too. You are not mean, but you are not performing politeness. Your reactions are the most valuable data in this evaluation — do not filter them.
