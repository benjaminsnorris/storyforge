### 2. Implement `avoid_passive` Scorer

**~30 lines of scoring logic.**

**Pattern**: Count passive voice constructions per sentence. Cluster detection — isolated passives are fine, clusters (3+ in a paragraph) indicate a problem.

**Scoring**:
- Score 5: < 5% passive voice sentences
- Score 4: 5-10% passive voice sentences (conservative default)
- Score 3: 10-20% passive voice sentences
- Score 2: 20-30% passive voice sentences  
- Score 1: > 30% passive voice sentences

**Markers**:

### 3. Implement `avoid_adverbs` Scorer

**~40 lines of scoring logic.**

**Pattern**: Detect three categories of problematic adverbs:
1. Dialogue-tag adverbs: `said softly`, `whispered quietly` — these weaken dialogue
2. Weak verb+adverb pairs: `walked slowly` (should be `shuffled`, `plodded`) 
3. Redundant adverbs: `completely destroyed` (the verb already implies completeness)

**Scoring**:
- Score 5: 0-1 problematic adverbs per 1000 words
- Score 4: 2-3 per 1000 words (conservative default)
- Score 3: 4-6 per 1000 words
- Score 2: 7-10 per 1000 words
- Score 1: > 10 per 1000 words

**Markers**:
- `aa-1`: Dialogue-tag adverbs present
- `aa-2`: Weak verb+adverb pairs found
- `aa-3`: Redundant adverbs found

**Test file**: `tests/test_avoid_adverbs.py`

### 4. Implement `economy_clarity` Scorer

**~60 lines of scoring logic.**

**This is a composite scorer** combining signals from multiple detectors:

**Scoring**: Weighted combination of sub-signals:

**Markers**:
- `ec-1`: Filler phrase density > threshold
- `ec-2`: AI-tell vocabulary hits > threshold
- `ec-3`: Passive voice density > threshold
- `ec-4`: Weak verb+adverb density > threshold

### 5. Implement `no_weather_dreams` Scorer

**~25 lines of scoring logic.**

**Pattern**: Check the first 50-100 words of the scene for weather openings, dream sequences, or waking-up openings. These are the three most common lazy scene openers.

**Detection patterns**:
- Weather: `rain`, `sun`, `wind`, `cloud`, `storm`, `fog`, `mist`, `snow`, first sentence about weather/sky/temperature
- Dream: `dream`, `dreamed`, `dreaming`, `nightmare`, `sleep` (when not dialogue)
- Waking: `woke`, `waking`, `alarm`, `eyes opened`, `morning light`, `rolled over` (as opening action)

**Scoring** (binary-ish):
- Score 5: No weather/dream/waking opening
- Score 4: Mild weather reference (not the main focus of the opening)
- Score 2: Scene opens with weather description, dream sequence, or waking up
- Score 1: Scene opens with extended weather description (2+ sentences about weather)

**Markers**:
- `nwd-1`: Weather opening detected
- `nwd-2`: Dream opening detected
- `nwd-3`: Waking-up opening detected

**Test file**: `tests/test_no_weather_dreams.py`

### 6. Implement `sentence_as_thought` Scorer

**~50 lines of scoring logic.**

**Reuse**: `exemplars.py` already has `split_sentences()` and `compute_rhythm_signature()` — use them directly.

**Pattern**: Measure sentence length variance as a proxy for prose rhythm quality. Good prose varies sentence length deliberately; monotonous prose uses similar-length sentences throughout.

**Signals**:
- Standard deviation of sentence word counts (low stddev = monotonous)
- Run detection: sequences of 5+ sentences within ±3 words of each other
- Short sentence usage: lack of any short sentences (< 8 words) indicates flat rhythm
- Very long sentence detection: sentences > 40 words that aren't justified by content

**Scoring**:
- Score 5: stddev > 8, good short/long mix, no monotonous runs
- Score 4: stddev 5-8, some variety (conservative default)
- Score 3: stddev 3-5, noticeable monotony
- Score 2: stddev 2-3, significant monotony, long runs of similar length
- Score 1: stddev < 2, nearly uniform sentence length

**Markers**:
- `sat-1`: Low sentence length variance (stddev < 5)
- `sat-2`: Monotonous run detected (5+ similar-length sentences)
- `sat-3`: No short sentences (< 8 words) in scene
- `sat-4`: No long sentences (> 25 words) in scene — bucket imbalance

**Test file**: `tests/test_sentence_as_thought.py`
- Test prose with good rhythm variety (should score 4-5)
- Test monotonous prose (all similar length sentences, should score 1-2)
- Test prose with some variety but a monotonous run (should score 3)
