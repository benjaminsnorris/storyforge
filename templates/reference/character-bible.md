## Primary Characters

---

### [Character Name]

**Role:** [Protagonist / Antagonist / etc.] Brief description of their function in the story.

**Physical:**
- Age, build, distinguishing features
- How they carry themselves
- Physical habits or tells (what does their body do when they are thinking, lying, afraid?)

**Psychological:**
- **Want:** What they consciously pursue
- **Need:** What they actually require (often different from the want)
- **Wound:** The formative injury that shaped them
- **Lie:** The false belief they carry because of the wound
- **Flaw-as-Strength:** The trait that is both their greatest asset and their greatest liability

**Voice Fingerprint:**
- Speech patterns (sentence length, vocabulary level, verbal habits)
- What they notice first when entering a room
- Metaphor domain (what field do they draw comparisons from -- engineering, nature, music, combat?)
- What they do not say (what topics they avoid, what emotions they suppress)

**Relational:**
- Key relationships and the nature of each (not just who, but what the dynamic is)
- How they behave differently with different people
- Who they trust, and why that trust is or is not earned

**Arc:**
1. Where they start (the opening state -- what the reader first sees)
2. Key turning points (the moments that change them)
3. Where they end (the closing state -- how they have been transformed)

**Expansion Opportunities:**
- Backstory threads worth developing
- Scenes or moments that could deepen the character
- Questions you have not yet answered about them
