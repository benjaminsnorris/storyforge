Modes

Three Scoring Modes

Choose the depth that fits the moment.

Grouped

4 calls per scene, one per craft section. The default mode — most accurate for systematic improvement.

Cost: ~$8-12 for 100 scenes

Quick

1 call per scene. Fast directional check — good for a pulse read after a round of revisions.

Cost: ~$2-3 for 100 scenes

Deep

23 calls per scene. Investigative mode — best combined with --scenes for targeted analysis.

Best for: targeted diagnosis

Tuning

The Five Tuning Levers

How the system translates scores into concrete improvements.

1
Craft Weights
Primary dials: 1-10 per principle. Injected into the writing prompt as a weighted directive — not the raw craft engine text.
2
Voice Guide
Concrete prose directives for when weight changes alone are not enough. "Shorten sentences in action beats" rather than "improve pacing."
3
Scene Intent
Sharpen the function and emotional_arc for specific scenes so the scorer and the writer are aligned on what each scene must do.
4
Per-Scene Overrides
Temporary instructions for specific scenes. Expire after the current cycle — designed for surgical, one-time fixes.
5
Tuning Ledger
Memory of what works and what doesn't. Each entry records the change made, the before/after scores, and whether it helped.

Calibration

Author Calibration

Teaching the system your taste.

Author Scoring

Authors can optionally provide their own scores for any scene. The delta between system and author scores teaches the system the author's personal taste — what they value that the generic rubric misses.

Exemplar Bank

Passages scoring 9+ are automatically collected as exemplars — concrete examples of "what excellence looks like in your voice." These exemplars are fed back into future writing prompts.

Scoring Rubrics — Every principle has defined score bands: 1-3 (fundamental problems), 4-6 (competent but unremarkable), 7-8 (skilled craft), 9-10 (exceptional). Each band includes literary exemplars from published works so the scorer has concrete reference points, not abstract ideals.
