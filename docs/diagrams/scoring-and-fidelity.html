Brief Fidelity

Did the prose deliver what the brief promised? Nine elements scored per scene.

Goal

Does the character pursue the stated objective?

Conflict

Is the stated opposition present and felt?

Outcome

Does the scene end as specified (yes/no/yes-but/no-and)?

Crisis

Does the character face the stated dilemma?

Decision

Does the character make the stated choice?

Key Actions

Are the specified actions present in the prose?

Key Dialogue

Do the specified lines appear (or close paraphrases)?

Emotions

Does the emotional sequence match the brief?

Knowledge

Does the character know the right things at start and end?

A score of 4 means the prose delivers the brief element clearly. A score of 5 means the prose found something better than what the brief specified. Low scores point to one of two problems: either the brief was right and the prose needs revision, or the prose is right and the brief needs updating.

How Findings Route

Evaluation findings are categorized by where the fix belongs — not all problems are prose problems.

1

Evaluate

Six expert perspectives read the manuscript. Each finding gets a fix_location tag.

2

Route Upstream

brief → fix scene-briefs.csv (knowledge, goal/conflict). intent → fix scene-intent.csv (value shifts, threads). structural → fix scenes.csv (POV, timeline).

3

Re-draft

Upstream CSV fixes trigger automatic re-drafting of affected scenes from updated briefs.

4

Polish

craft findings go directly to prose polish — voice, rhythm, naturalness. No upstream changes needed.
