The Evaluation Panel

Six expert perspectives read the manuscript. Their findings are categorized and routed to the right fix location — upstream CSV or prose.

Six Perspectives

Developmental Editor

"Does the story work?"

Structure, character arcs, plot coherence, pacing, thematic integrity.

Line Editor

"Does the prose work?"

Sentence craft, rhythm, word choice, dialogue, AI pattern detection.

Genre Expert

"Does it deliver the genre promise?"

Convention adherence, trope deployment, reader expectation fulfillment.

Literary Agent

"Can I sell this?"

Market positioning, hook strength, comp titles, query pitch potential.

First Reader

"Would I keep reading?"

Page-turner quality, emotional impact, confusion points, character investment.

Writing Coach

"How is the author growing?"

Signature strengths, habitual weaknesses, ambition vs execution gap.

Evaluation Flow

1

Parallel Evaluation

All six evaluators read the manuscript simultaneously via Batch API. Each produces an independent report.

2

Synthesis

A reconciliation pass combines all reports: consensus findings, contested points, prioritized action items, strengths to protect.

3

Categorize

Each finding gets a fix_location tag: where the fix belongs.

Finding Routing

Not all problems are prose problems. Findings route to the right layer.

structural

Fix scenes.csv (POV, timeline, parts)

intent

Fix scene-intent.csv (value shifts, threads)

brief

Fix scene-briefs.csv (knowledge, goal/conflict)

craft

Fix prose directly (voice, rhythm)

Upstream fixes (structural, intent, brief) trigger automatic re-drafting of affected scenes from the updated CSV data. Craft fixes go to the polish pass. This means structural problems are fixed at their source, not patched in prose.
