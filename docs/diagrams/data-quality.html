Data Quality & Diagnostics

Fix problems as data edits before they become prose rewrites. One command to diagnose, targeted domains to fix.

Diagnose

./storyforge hone --diagnose

Read-only assessment. Runs structural scoring, brief quality detection, and gap analysis in one pass. No API calls, no files modified.

  • 8 structural dimensions (0-1 scores)
  • Brief quality issues (per field)
  • Gap detection (missing required fields)
  • Clean scene count

Hone

./storyforge hone --domain briefs

Fix what diagnose found. Four domains target different categories of data quality problems. Run one domain at a time or all at once.

  • registries — normalize field values
  • briefs — concretize abstract language
  • gaps — fill missing required fields
  • structural — apply evaluation fixes

Brief Quality Issues

The briefs domain detects four categories of field-level problems that hurt drafting quality.

Abstract Language

Thematic or narrator-voice descriptions instead of physical action. The drafter can't translate "the realization building" into concrete prose without inventing the scene.

Abstract: "the parallel crystallizing; the tension deepening"
Concrete: "her hands stop on the map; the room goes quiet"

Over-Specified Beats

Too many key_actions for the scene's word count. Five beats in 1,500 words means one mandatory event every 300 words — no room for atmosphere or interiority.

Flagged: 7 beats in 1,200 target words (171 words/beat)
Target: 2-3 beats per 2,500 words

Verbose Fields

Fields that became paragraph summaries instead of terse beat lists. Common after extraction, where Claude reverse-engineers structure from prose and writes field values that read like summaries.

Verbose decision (225 chars): "They hold. Naji breaks her deal and stands with the community. The Hunter kills her..."
Terse decision (27 chars): "She goes through the door"

Gaps

Empty required fields for the scene's pipeline status. A scene at "briefed" status without a goal, conflict, or crisis can't be drafted coherently.

Detected: scene at "briefed" status missing: conflict, crisis, decision

Structural Scoring

Eight dimensions scored deterministically from CSV data alone — no API calls, pure pattern matching.

Arc Completeness

Do protagonist arcs show meaningful value reversals across the full manuscript?

Thematic Concentration

Are values at stake focused on a core set, or scattered across dozens of one-off themes?

Pacing Shape

Does tension escalate through the manuscript? Is the climax positioned correctly?

Character Presence

Are major characters on-stage consistently, or do they vanish for long stretches?

MICE Thread Health

Are threads opened and closed in proper nesting order? Are any dormant too long?

Knowledge Chain

Does information flow logically from scene to scene? Are there knowledge violations?

Function Variety

Is there a healthy mix of scene types, or does one outcome dominate?

Structural Completeness

Are required fields populated? Are enrichment fields filled for scenes that need them?

The Workflow

1

Diagnose

Run ./storyforge hone --diagnose to see the full picture: structural scores, brief quality issues, gaps. No changes, just a report.

2

Prioritize

Gaps first (scenes can't be drafted without briefs), then brief quality (overspecified beats, verbose fields), then registries (normalization).

3

Fix

Run hone on specific domains: --domain briefs to concretize, --domain gaps to fill, --domain registries to normalize. Scope with --act 2 or --scenes id,id.

4

Verify

Re-run diagnose to confirm improvements. The dashboard also shows brief quality on the spine (colored underlines) and in a dedicated grid on the Scores page.
