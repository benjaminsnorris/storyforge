Write novels with structural integrity

Storyforge helps you build the bones of your story before you write a word of prose — so your first draft is dramatically better.

The problem with drafting first

Most novelists write their way into the story, then spend months fixing what they find: continuity errors, dropped subplots, characters who know things they shouldn't, a middle that sags because the structure wasn't there to hold it up.

Storyforge takes a different approach. Build the structural skeleton first — scene by scene, validated at every step — so that when you write prose, the foundation is already sound. Continuity, knowledge flow, pacing, and thread management are checked before they become prose problems.

How it works

You start with a seed — a logline, a character, a thematic obsession. From there, Storyforge guides you through progressively deeper levels of structural development:

Spine

The 5–10 events that must happen. Your protagonist's wound, lie, and need. The three-level conflict. The ending.

Architecture

Expand to a full scene structure. Assign POV, track value shifts, classify action vs. sequel rhythm, map story threads.

Scene Map

Every scene gets a location, timeline position, character list, and thread tracking. The full spatial and temporal picture of your novel.

Briefs

The drafting contract per scene: goal, conflict, outcome, crisis, decision. Who knows what entering and leaving. Key actions and dialogue.

At each stage, automatic validation catches problems: timeline inconsistencies, knowledge violations, dropped threads, flat pacing stretches. You fix these as simple data edits — before they're baked into thousands of words of prose.

When you're ready to draft, every scene has a validated brief. Scenes can even be written in parallel because their knowledge dependencies are mapped. The result: a first draft with structural integrity already built in.

Already have a manuscript?

Storyforge can read your existing prose and extract the structural data — character knowledge, value shifts, thread tracking, scene purposes — revealing what's working and where the problems are. Authors use this to understand their own manuscripts at a structural level that's genuinely hard to see from inside the draft.

Three ways to work

You choose how much Storyforge does and how much you do. This isn't a dial — it's a different role for your AI collaborator.

Full

Creative Partner

Storyforge proposes, builds, drafts. You review and redirect. You could be surprised by creative choices — the structural guardrails ensure quality.

Coach

Dramaturg

Storyforge presents options and asks sharp questions. You make every creative decision. It structures and challenges — never generates prose.

Strict

Continuity Editor

You write everything. Storyforge runs exhaustive validation — catching knowledge violations, thread drops, and timeline errors across 50+ scenes.

Grounded in craft

Every structural decision Storyforge tracks maps to an established craft principle. McKee's value shifts tell you if your scenes turn. Swain's action/sequel pattern reveals your pacing rhythm. Kowal's MICE threads track your subplot nesting. Story Grid's Five Commandments ensure every scene has a genuine crisis and an active decision.

This isn't a black box. It's a system you can learn from — and the more you understand the principles, the more powerful the tool becomes.
